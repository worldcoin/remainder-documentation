The Remainder Book

Hyrax Primitives

We go over various sigma protocols (interactive proofs with just 3 rounds of interaction) that allow the prover to prove various statements on its committed messages without having to open the commitment. For all of these protocols, let gi​∈G be the message commitment generators, and h∈G be the blinding generator. Let Fp​ be the scalar field of G. Assume the prover produces the blinding factor r∈Fp​ using a cryptographic PRNG.

Proof of Opening

In a Proof of Opening, P shows that given a commitment C0​=xg+rh, P knows the message x and blinding factor r used to generate this commitment.

  1. P→V: P samples t1​,t2​←$Fp​ uniformly from the scalar field. P computes and sends over α=t1​g+t2​h.
  2. V→P: A random challenge c from Fp​.
  3. P→V: z1​=x⋅c+t1​, and z2​=r⋅c+t2​.
  4. V checks: z1​g+z2​h=?cC0​+α.

Proof of Equality

In a Proof of Equality, P convinces V that two commitments C0​=v0​g+s0​h and C1​=v1​g+s1​h commit to the same value, i.e. v0​=v1​. In other words P knows that v0​=v1​, but V only has the blinded commitments, which look uniformly random (since s0​h,s1​h are uniformly randomly distributed in G for uniformly random s0​,s1​∈Fp​).

  1. P→V: P first uniformly samples a random value r from Fp​. P computes α=rh and sends to V.
  2. V→P: A random challenge c from Fp​.
  3. P→V: z=c⋅(s0​−s1​)+r
  4. V checks: zh=?c(C0​−C1​)+α.

Proof of Product

Proof of product shows that a commitment Z=zg+rZ​h is a commitment to the product of the messages committed to in X=xg+rX​h and Y=yg+rY​h. In other words, P knows that x⋅y=z and wants to prove this to V without revealing the messages and just using the commitments.

  1. P→V: P uniformly samples b1​,…,b5​ from Fp​ and computes and sends over α=b1​g+b2​h,β=b3​g+b4​h,δ=b3​X+b5​h.
  2. V→P: A random challenge c from Fp​.
  3. P→V: z1​=b1​+c⋅xz2​=b2​+c⋅rX​z3​=b3​+c⋅yz4​=b4​+c⋅rY​z5​=b5​+c⋅(rZ​−rX​y)
  4. V checks: α+cX=?z1​g+z2​hβ+cY=?z3​g+z4​hδ+cZ=?z3​X+z5​h
